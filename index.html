Global Cure Simulator

Game Overview

You are the leader of a global health organization attempting to cure a disease spreading across the entire population. At the start of the game everyone is infected. Each turn your organization cures a percentage of the remaining infected population.

This simulation demonstrates exponential decay. If the cure rate is b, then each turn the infected population becomes:

I(t+1) = I(t) × (1 − b)

Increasing the cure rate causes the infected population to shrink faster. You can purchase initiatives to improve treatment capacity, increase funding, or manage public perception.

Important systems:

  • Budget grows each turn through interest.
  • Initiatives modify cure rate, funding, or reputation.
  • Reputation decreases when many people remain infected.
  • If reputation reaches 0, the organization collapses.
  • If the infected population reaches 0, you win.

Watch the graph to see how your decisions affect the exponential decay curve.
